HUMAN CLASS
EQUIPMENT · STAT ITEMS · MAX VALUES · OPTIMIZATION

CLASS OVERVIEW

Humans are entirely equipment-dependent. They gain no stats from winning battles — growth comes exclusively from consumable stat items found in shops and dungeons. What they lack in natural growth they compensate for with 8 equipment slots — the most of any class — allowing significant weapon and armor stacking.

EQUIPMENT SLOTS: 8

Can carry 8 weapons/items simultaneously. Equipping all 5 armor pieces (helmet, chest, gloves, shoes, shield-equivalent) still leaves 3 weapon slots.

ARMOR USE

Humans are the only class (with Mutants) that can equip armor. Full armor set stacks significant DEF bonuses on top of base stats.

NO NATURAL GROWTH

Winning battles does nothing for a Human's stats. Only consumable stat items (Strong, Agility, etc.) permanently raise base stats.

STAT ITEMS

Permanent stat increases come from consuming specific items. These are one-use items found in shops (at high prices) and occasionally in dungeons.

A Human's greatest defensive advantage comes from stacking all 5 armor pieces. A full Dragon-tier armor set can add 45+ DEF, making the Human extremely tanky against physical attacks.

ARMOR SYSTEM

Armor has 5 piece types. Each type provides a different bonus. Humans (and Mutants) can equip one of each type. Armor takes up equipment slots.

WEAPON STRATEGY

Humans should carry at least 2–3 weapons at all times. Weapons have limited uses and will break mid-dungeon. The safest loadout for a Human:

SLOT ALLOCATION

5 armor pieces + 3 weapons is a typical distribution.

Or: skip some armor and carry more weapons for longer dungeon runs.

MAIN WEAPON

Use the strongest sword available for your world tier. Dragon/Sun swords are best before the Tower; Masamune is end-game best.

BACKUP

Always carry a gun as backup. Guns bypass DEF and don't run out of uses as quickly as swords. Essential for Tower of God enemies with very high DEF.
